My Teaching Philosophy

"Learning is the breeze that turns a quiet ember into a roaring fire."

A teacher's approach to education is based on the premise that students have different strengths, interests and styles of learning. A teacher strives to establish a safe, supported classroom environment in which all students can seek knowledge, take risks and develop their own curiosity.

As a teacher, I focuses on developing critical-thinking skills rather than rote memorization; thus, developing a student's ability to comprehend, analyze and synthesize information that prepares for the experiences of everyday living beyond the confines of the classroom. This goal is accomplished by providing opportunities for students to apply concepts in the "real world" using project-based learning, allowing for a connection between what is learned in school and how students may live/dream.

Technology has the propensity, if used properly, to differentiate learning opportunities and to enhance the way students become engaged in learning situations. The purpose of technology is not to replace traditional teaching methods, but rather to augment them by providing students with an array of options in which to personalise their learning experience and collaborate with other students.

The most important attitude that can be formed is that of desire to go on learning. This is the foundation of all true education.

- John Dewey
