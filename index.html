Network Types and Setups

Usually, networks are grouped by size. Since you're running a small business in one place, you'll probably want a Local Area Network (LAN). A LAN hooks up your computers and devices in a small area, like your office or even your house. It's quick for sending files and keeps your stuff safe (Borodin, 2024). When it comes to how things are laid out, which is called topology, there are some ways to do it. A Bus setup uses one main cable, and a Ring setup makes a loop. But for you, I'd go with a Star setup. This is where everything plugs into one central box, which is a switch. It's beneficial for small businesses because if one cable breaks, only that computer goes down, not the entire system. Additionally, it's straightforward to add more computers as your team grows (Lowe, 2020). Connecting Devices Explained Simply Think of these devices as helping control traffic in your office between computers. The main things you'll see are hubs, switches, and routers: Hubs: These are older and not that smart. A hub sends any data it gets to every computer on the network. This makes extra work and can cause security problems (White, 2022). Switches: This is what we'll use for your LAN. A switch is smarter than a hub. It figures out which computer is plugged into which port and only sends data to the right one. That way, your internal stuff moves fast and smoothly. Routers: Switches hook up your computers to each other. A router hooks up your whole network to the internet. It's like a doorway, sending traffic between your office network and the rest of the world (Stallings, 2021). How Data Moves Simplex: Data only goes one way, like a one-way street. A keyboard sending info to your computer is like this. It's not often used for people talking to each other at work. Half-Duplex: Data can go both ways, but only one direction at a time—think of walkie-talkies. If two people talk at once, you can't hear either one. Some older Wi-Fi staff do this. Full-Duplex: This is great for offices now. Data moves both ways at the same time, like a phone call (Kurose & Ross, 2021). People can upload and download without slowing down. My Recommendation For your small business, I think a Star-topology LAN using Full-Duplex switches and a solid Router to get online is the best bet. This makes sure your network is strong, can grow as you do, and is fast enough for today's programs (Borodin, 2024; Lowe, 2020; Stallings, 2021). If you get a switch instead of a hub and make sure your stuff does full duplex, you'll stop your office from having those data traffic jams that can mess up networks that aren't set up well.
